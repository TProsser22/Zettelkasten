## 5325.25.25e — Intellectualism like Scientism — a Critique

Per the author, Intellectualism is polluted by the belief you can separate an action from its results; you can separate theory from practice; and you can fix a complex system with hierarchical approaches in a top down manner. Similarly, Scientism interprets Science merely as a complication instead of as a process and a skeptical enterprise. Scientism:

- using math when not needed
- replacing your well-functioning hand with technology
- replacing "natural, age-old" processes that survived trillions of high-dimensional stressors with a "peer-reviewed" journal that may not [withstand] replication or statistical scrutiny.

This is neither good science or practice.

At this time the author states science has been taken over by vendors and ironically, the skeptical enterprise is being used to silence skeptics!

> 📚 *Taleb, N (2018) pg. 27*
