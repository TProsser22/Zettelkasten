## 4204.50.40 — Ergodicity

The probabilities of success from a group do NOT apply to an individual. The group is an ensemble probability and the individual experiences the risk with a time probability. "In ensemble probability the ruin of one individual does not affect the others. In a time probability, ruin affects all future points in time." ^pg.5^ The individual's experience is non-ergodic. Pretty much every human system is non-ergodic! See Financial Sequence of Return Risk `2323A.50.50`. Mathematically, the properties of a very, very long sample path will look like Monte Carlo calculated properties of shorter paths (on average). ^pg.92^ Beware of non-ergodic systems as randomness (variability) can cause blow-ups.

> 📚 *Pearson, T (2019) PDF pgs. 5–6*
> 📚 *Taleb, N (2016) pg. 92*
