## 5323.25.23 — Complexity vs Complicated

Complex issues or systems cannot be characterized by any rules, algorithms, or natural laws (as in physics).

Complicated has discernable order & cause and effect relationships that allow you to reproduce results.

To be complex is to have no such degree of order, control or predictability. They don't have obvious one-dimensional causes-and-effect mechanisms. The ensemble does not behave in a way predictable by its components. Interactions are more significant than the actions & characteristics of the units. ^69^ Complex systems are opaque; it is very risky to mess with them.

> 📚 *Taleb, N (2018) pg. 9 & 69*
> 📚 *Nason, R (2017) Preface*

## 5323.25.25 — Complex Systems Pose Hidden Dangers

According to Taleb, complex systems do not have one-dimensional cause & effect mechanisms (even if we would like to attribute simple explanations). Therefore it is very dangerous to mess with them under our limited understanding due to opacity. "The curse of dimensionality."

Decentralization can reduce structural asymmetries of large complex systems, moving decisions & risks closer to the problem and reducing some of the opacity.

> 🔗 *see also: Reverse-engineering Evolution 7120.1A.2*
> 📚 *Taleb, N (2018) pgs. 9–12, 89*

## 5323.25.25/1b — Fragility

"That which is fragile has an asymmetric response to volatility and other stressors, that is, it will experience more harm than benefit" from stress or volatility. Fragility = Poor Risk Mgmt structure. This is the opposite of Taleb's Antifragile where shocks, stress, & volatility strengthen the entity. Fragility mathematically translates into exposure to fat tails — rare but devastating risks.

> 📚 *Taleb, N (2018) pg. 142*

## 5323.25.25/3 — Survival

"What is rational is what allows the collective — entities meant to live for a long time — to survive."

"Not everything that happens, happens for a reason; but everything that survives, survives for a reason." Taleb's entire "survival-as-a-filter" argument is fundamentally about managing exposure to risk over time — not just surviving one-off events, but building systems, behaviors, or heuristics that survive repeated volatility.

> 📚 *Taleb, N (2018) pgs. 26, 221*

## 5323.25.25/3c — Religion as Intergenerational Tail-risk Mgmt.

Religion is a risk-mitigation system, designed to mitigate existential tail risks that humans cannot individually calculate by encoding heuristics that protect against systemic & catastrophic risks. Religion provides memetic survival advantages scaling individual behaviors into collective strategies. "Its binary & unconditional rules are easy to teach & enforce."

> 📚 *Taleb, N (2018) pg. 217*

## 5323.25.26 — Systemic Risk

This is the heart of Risk Mgmt. It is the risk to an entire system, where failure is multiplicative, contagious, and irreversable. You cannot manage this by calculating averages; you have to avoid fragility (see 5323.25.25/1b). "All risks are not equal." Never compare a multiplicative, systemic, & fat-tailed risk to a non-cascading, non-scaling, idiosyncratic, thin-tailed one.

> 📚 *Taleb, N (2018) pg. 231*

## 5323.25.27 — Curse of Dimensionality

A disproportionate increase in computational demands as the number of interactions increase. The more dimensions of interaction the more difficult to understand the macro from the micro. Even if we understood the actions of a[n] cell's components we could not explain the cell in its entirety. The ensemble behaves in ways [not] predicted by the components.

> 📚 *Taleb, N (2018) pgs. 69 & 89*

## 5323.50.50 — Beware of Self-organizing Fraud

Following investigations of the Financial crisis (2007) scandals, the author coined the term "Self-organizing Fraud." Fraudulent activities emerge and eluove [sic] in complex organizations without individuals or entities being directly responsible. Complexity and accountability sinks in the environment, along with unstated incentives that were criminogenic. In the Finance world (banks in particular), top executives set unrealistic profitability targets while underinvesting in regulatory & compliance controls and magically the banks organized themselves into fraudulent behaviour.

Reg design neglect + bad incentives = crime

> 📚 *Davies, D (2024) pg. 12*
