## 5000A4-9.10.25 — Information Scarcity Breeds Authority

When information is rare, gated, or controlled, the few sources that do distribute it (e.g. media, govt.) are automatically deemed credible. There used to be no competition prior to the information age or what Martin Gurri called "The Fifth Wave," no challenge to the Centers' narrative. The old media & government authority was a direct product of their monopoly on information. "A curious thing happens to sources of info under conditions of scarcity. They become authoritative." ^20^

> 🔗 *see also: 1501.3.4*
> 📚 *Gurri, M (2018) pg. 20*

## 5000F.3.3 — 4 Elements of Tragic Social Engineering by the State

1. Administrative ordering of nature & society — attempts at state simplifications
2. High-Modernist ideology — see `5000F.5.5`

"Only when these two elements are combined to the 3rd does the combination become potentially lethal."

3. An authoritarian state with the coercive power necessary to bring the above 2 elements into reality. Typically emerging during times of war, revolution, depression, or the struggle for national liberation.
4. A prostrate civil society lacking the ability to resist.

> 📚 *Scott, J (2020) pgs. 5–6*

## 5000F.3.3c — Simplifications (Dangers of)

Simplifying complex systems or high volatility probabilistic environments is not possible without severe distortion and eventually severe consequences. Simplification is antithetical to complexity. In environments or systems with high dimensionality — "Keep it simple stupid" is highly dangerous. ^33^ Of course, one should simplify as best you can to get across your point, but some things are too complex to fit into a media soundbite. Simplifying in financial markets can lead to a "blowup." ^70^ Simplification can be a valuable cognitive tool, but if relied on too much can expose you to risks.

> 🔗 *see also: State Simplifications 5000F.3.4*
> 📚 *Taleb, N (2016) pgs. 33, 70*

## 5000F.3.4 — State Simplifications

Requiring a central view, the State attempts to simplify the complexity of the world much like a map-maker, losing a tremendous amount of detail and particularity. They are:

- Simplified observations of only those aspects of social life of official interest.
- Written documentary facts
- Static facts
- Aggregated facts
- Facts capable of standardization showing differences along a standardized scale or continuum.
- A mechanical application of generic rules that ignore important particularities ^pg.318^

> 📚 *Scott, J (2020) pgs. 80–87, 318*

## 5000F.5.5 — High Modernist Ideology

Another way of saying progressivism of technocratic rule. Puts scientific and technical progress above all, often leading to authoritarianism and coercion.

- A claim to improving the human condition using the authority of scientific knowledge and excluding other sources of judgement ^pg.98^
- A deeply authoritarian view that scientifically designed schemes are superior to traditional ways. ^pg.94^
- Reflexive contempt for history and past knowledge ^pg.305^

**Summary Quote:** "A strong, muscle-bound version of the self-confident belief in scientific & technical progress, the expansion of production, the growing satisfaction of human needs, the mastery of nature (incl. human nature), and above all, the rational design of social order."

> 📚 *Scott, J (2020) pgs. 94–98, 305, 342*
